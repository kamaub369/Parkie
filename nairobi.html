🦁 Nairobi National Park: Wildlife at the Heart of the City

Published on: June 23, 2025 by Parktime Tours

Where else can you spot lions with skyscrapers in the background? Nairobi National Park is the world’s only national park located within a capital city — offering a surreal blend of wildlife and urban life.

🌆 A Safari Just Minutes from the CBD

Just 7 km from Nairobi’s bustling city center, Nairobi National Park is a haven for over 100 mammal species and 400+ bird species. It’s an ideal escape for travelers on a tight schedule or residents looking for a wild weekend.

🐘 What You’ll See

  • Lions & Rhinos: One of Kenya’s best places to spot endangered black rhinos and big cats.
  • Giraffes, Buffalos & Zebras: Roam freely against the city skyline.
  • Birds: Migratory storks, vultures, and eagles make this park a birder’s paradise.

🌿 Things to Do

  • Take a half-day game drive with a local guide.
  • Visit the David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage nearby.
  • Explore the Nairobi Safari Walk and Animal Orphanage.
  • Pack a picnic and relax at designated sites within the park.

📅 Best Time to Visit

Nairobi National Park is open year-round. For the best animal sightings, go during the dry season (June–October & January–February) when grass is shorter and animals are more visible.

🚌 Accessibility

You can reach the park in under 30 minutes from most parts of Nairobi. It's accessible by private car, tour van, or taxi.

📸 Don’t Miss This Shot

Capture a photo of giraffes walking across the savannah with Nairobi’s skyline in the background — it’s a signature image that defines Kenya’s unique balance between nature and modernity.
